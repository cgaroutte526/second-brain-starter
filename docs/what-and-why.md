# What is this — and why?

A **Second Brain** is a folder of plain notes on your computer that Claude Code reads and writes for you. It remembers your projects, people, and decisions across sessions — so instead of re-explaining your work every time, Claude already knows it.

## What changes about your day
- **Mornings:** run `/today` → a ranked plan built from your real projects and yesterday's loose ends, slotted into your calendar.
- **Writing:** run `/draft` → a first draft in *your* voice, grounded in your actual project context — not generic AI filler.
- **End of day:** run `/shutdown` → 5 minutes; it captures what got done and sets up tomorrow. This is what makes it get smarter over time.

## The five commands
`/setup` (once, builds your vault) · `/today` (morning plan) · `/draft` (write things) · `/ingest raw` (pull files in) · `/shutdown` (close the day).

## The honest cost
- ~30 minutes to set up (the `/setup` interview does most of it).
- ~3 hours, spread out, to get all your projects loaded.
- One real habit: run `/shutdown` most days. That's the engine.

## What you need
- A work computer (Windows or Mac).
- Claude Code (free to install; uses your company Claude Pro plan).
- 15 minutes to start. See the quickstart for your OS.

If that sounds useful, open the quickstart for **Windows** or **Mac** and you'll be running in about 15 minutes.
